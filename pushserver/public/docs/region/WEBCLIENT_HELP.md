Welcome!
========

You're about to step into the world's first MMO, Lucasfilm's Habitat! There are thousands
of regions to explore and many wonderful folks to meet from all around the world who come to
visit every day.

This is the **Web Client** — Habitat reborn in your browser. There's no emulator to start and
no disks to flip: it loads in seconds and runs at modern speed.

Logging In
----------

When the title screen appears, **click to begin**. In a moment an **Enter Habitat** button shows
up — **press any key** (or click it) and you'll be asked to type a name for your new **Avatar**,
such as **Threepwood**, **dril**, or **Phil Collins**. That's it — you'll wake up inworld a moment
later.

Moving and Doing
----------------

Your cursor is a Habitat joystick. **Press and hold** on the scene, then **nudge** in a direction
to choose an action — the cursor changes color to show which one — and **release** to carry it out:

| Nudge  | Action | Cursor |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Up     | **GO** — walk there       | green  |
| Down   | **DO** — use / operate it | yellow |
| Right  | **GET** — pick it up       | red    |
| Left   | **PUT** — set it down      | blue   |

A quick **tap** with no nudge simply turns your Avatar to face that spot. To pick up or operate
something, aim at the object before you release.

Talking
-------

Just **type** — with your real keyboard or the on-screen one — and your words appear in a word
balloon over your Avatar's head. Tap the **keyboard icon** (⌨) to show or hide the on-screen
keyboard; it's handy on touch screens and kiosks. The **F-keys** trigger gestures and special
actions (for example, **F3** lists who else is here in the region).

Traveling
---------

Click the **arrow chevrons** (◀ ▶ ▼) around the edges of the scene to walk into the neighboring
region. The **compass** and the four region previews along the bottom show you which way each
exit leads, so you'll always know where you're headed.

The Docent
----------

This panel is your **Docent** — NeoHabitat's interactive guide. As you travel, it fills in with
helpful tips, dynamic help, and historical background for the world around you. You can return to
this page anytime by clicking the **Help** button.
